Project Overview

Students from over 50 countries attend Penn State Harrisburg. As a result, me and a group of 4 decided to design a mobile application for international students to find American English idioms that may be complex or difficult to understand. After defining our target group to be ESL individuals, we used affinity diagrams and the KJ method (tools used to generate and organize ideas amongst a design team) to create the functionalities for the app. We utilized hierarchal task analysis to map out the user's journey. We then created wireframes by hand and created them in Figma. Testing of the high-fidelity prototype occured using Loop11. We recruited 5 participants within the ESL community to test our prototype and found that the mobile app was well-designed and a decent product, yet rather simplistic in nature, and could include more depth of features to make it of of maximum benefit to international and ESL students. My redesign after implementing changes is the product pictured on this portfolio.

Research

I led a study with 5 ESL students using contextual inquiries to gain insights on ESL students' processes when they use the internet to look up idioms.
